Nicaragua

See for yourself
Just grab a swimsuit and some sneakers!

Nicaragua is a Central American paradise,known for its beaches, volcanoes, and islands. The country has a population of 6 million residents, and the national language is spanish. There are a variety of activities like zip-lining, visiting volcanoes at night, kayaking, surfing, and of course eating yummy food!

Ready to go?
Volcan Mombacho!

Volcan Mombacho is located near Granada, the last eruption occurred in 1570. The volcano is filled with beautiful vegetation and an amazing flourishing ecosystem.

The volcano provides for a day of adventure! You can hike into different senderos or try out your monkey skills with zip-lining! Learn more here.

Now drive a few hours for a different volcano experience!

Volcano Boarding!

Ride a wooden sled down a steep, active volcano! Cerro Negro is 161 years old and has small, penny-size volcanic rock surrounding the volcano, perfect sliding conditions! Don't forget to bring your Go-Pro!

My Personal Favorite...

Granada

A beautiful colonial city surrounding Lake Nicaragua, also known as a tropical Spain, the city was founded in 1524 and each building is incredibly different, each with its own striking beauty. Full of artists, the city is small and cute, and you can walk it all! Overflowing with culture, shows and food head over to La Calzada. A famous, energetic street! Hungry? Check out these local favorites.

  • Restaurante El Zaguan
  • La Claraboya
  • Bistro Estrada
